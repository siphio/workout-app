# Clean Gains Workout Program

A Push/Pull/Legs (PPL) program optimized for progressive overload and muscle growth.

---

## Program Overview

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Split | Push / Pull / Legs / Rest (Repeat) |
| Frequency | Each muscle group 2× per week |
| Sessions per week | 6 training days, 2 rest days |
| Session duration | 45-60 minutes |
| Focus | Hypertrophy with strength foundation |

### Weekly Schedule Example

```
Week 1:
Mon: Push    Tue: Pull    Wed: Legs    Thu: Rest
Fri: Push    Sat: Pull    Sun: Legs

Week 2:
Mon: Rest    Tue: Push    Wed: Pull    Thu: Legs
Fri: Rest    Sat: Push    Sun: Pull

(Continues rolling based on completion)
```

## Push Day

**Target Muscles:** Chest, Shoulders, Triceps

| # | Exercise | Sets | Reps | Rest | Notes |
|---|----------|------|------|------|-------|
| 1 | Barbell Bench Press | 4 | 6-8 | 3 min | Compound strength focus |
| 2 | Seated Dumbbell Shoulder Press | 3 | 8-10 | 2 min | Strict form, no leg drive |
| 3 | Incline Dumbbell Press | 3 | 8-12 | 90 sec | 30-45° angle |
| 4 | Cable Lateral Raises | 3 | 12-15 | 60 sec | Control the negative |
| 5 | Tricep Pushdowns (Rope) | 3 | 10-12 | 60 sec | Squeeze at bottom |
| 6 | Overhead Tricep Extension | 3 | 10-12 | 60 sec | Full stretch at bottom |

### Push Day Details

#### 1. Barbell Bench Press
- **Primary:** Chest
- **Secondary:** Front delts, triceps
- **Cues:** 
  - Retract shoulder blades
  - Arch upper back slightly
  - Touch mid-chest
  - Drive feet into floor
- **Progression:** Add 2.5kg when you hit 4×8

#### 2. Seated Dumbbell Shoulder Press
- **Primary:** Front & side delts
- **Secondary:** Triceps
- **Cues:**
  - Keep core tight
  - Don't flare elbows too wide
  - Full lockout at top
- **Progression:** Add 2kg when you hit 3×10

#### 3. Incline Dumbbell Press
- **Primary:** Upper chest
- **Secondary:** Front delts, triceps
- **Cues:**
  - Set bench to 30-45°
  - Bring dumbbells to outer chest
  - Press in slight arc
- **Progression:** Add 2kg when you hit 3×12

#### 4. Cable Lateral Raises
- **Primary:** Side delts
- **Cues:**
  - Slight lean away from cable
  - Lead with elbow
  - Control the descent (3 sec negative)
- **Progression:** Add 2.5kg when you hit 3×15

#### 5. Tricep Pushdowns (Rope)
- **Primary:** Triceps (lateral head)
- **Cues:**
  - Keep elbows pinned to sides
  - Spread rope at bottom
  - Squeeze for 1 second
- **Progression:** Add 2.5kg when you hit 3×12

#### 6. Overhead Tricep Extension
- **Primary:** Triceps (long head)
- **Cues:**
  - Keep elbows pointed forward
  - Full stretch at bottom
  - Can use rope, EZ bar, or dumbbell
- **Progression:** Add 2.5kg when you hit 3×12

## Pull Day

**Target Muscles:** Back, Rear Delts, Biceps

| # | Exercise | Sets | Reps | Rest | Notes |
|---|----------|------|------|------|-------|
| 1 | Barbell Deadlift | 4 | 5-6 | 3-4 min | Strength focus, reset each rep |
| 2 | Weighted Pull-Ups | 4 | 6-10 | 2-3 min | Or Lat Pulldown if needed |
| 3 | Barbell Bent Over Row | 3 | 8-10 | 2 min | Overhand grip, 45° torso |
| 4 | Face Pulls | 3 | 15-20 | 60 sec | External rotation at top |
| 5 | Barbell Curls | 3 | 8-10 | 60 sec | No swinging |
| 6 | Hammer Curls | 3 | 10-12 | 60 sec | Alternating or together |

### Pull Day Details

#### 1. Barbell Deadlift
- **Primary:** Posterior chain (hamstrings, glutes, erectors)
- **Secondary:** Lats, traps, forearms
- **Cues:**
  - Bar over mid-foot
  - Chest up, back flat
  - Push floor away
  - Lock out with glutes, not lower back
- **Progression:** Add 5kg when you hit 4×6

#### 2. Weighted Pull-Ups
- **Primary:** Lats
- **Secondary:** Biceps, rear delts
- **Cues:**
  - Full dead hang at bottom
  - Pull elbows to hips
  - Chin over bar
  - Control the descent
- **Substitution:** Lat Pulldown if can't do 4×6 bodyweight
- **Progression:** Add 2.5kg when you hit 4×10

#### 3. Barbell Bent Over Row
- **Primary:** Upper back, lats
- **Secondary:** Rear delts, biceps
- **Cues:**
  - Hinge at hips, 45° torso angle
  - Pull to lower chest/upper abs
  - Squeeze shoulder blades
  - Control the negative
- **Progression:** Add 2.5kg when you hit 3×10

#### 4. Face Pulls
- **Primary:** Rear delts, rotator cuff
- **Secondary:** Traps
- **Cues:**
  - Set cable at face height
  - Pull to ears, not chin
  - Externally rotate at end (hands above elbows)
  - Pause at contraction
- **Progression:** Add 2.5kg when you hit 3×20

#### 5. Barbell Curls
- **Primary:** Biceps (short head)
- **Cues:**
  - Keep elbows pinned
  - No swinging or momentum
  - Full extension at bottom
  - Squeeze at top
- **Progression:** Add 2.5kg when you hit 3×10

#### 6. Hammer Curls
- **Primary:** Biceps (long head), brachialis
- **Cues:**
  - Neutral grip throughout
  - Keep elbows stationary
  - Can do alternating or simultaneous
- **Progression:** Add 2kg when you hit 3×12

## Legs Day

**Target Muscles:** Quads, Hamstrings, Glutes, Calves

| # | Exercise | Sets | Reps | Rest | Notes |
|---|----------|------|------|------|-------|
| 1 | Barbell Back Squat | 4 | 6-8 | 3 min | High bar, full depth |
| 2 | Romanian Deadlift (RDL) | 3 | 8-10 | 2 min | Feel hamstring stretch |
| 3 | Leg Press | 3 | 10-12 | 2 min | Feet shoulder width |
| 4 | Leg Curl (Lying or Seated) | 3 | 10-12 | 90 sec | Squeeze at contraction |
| 5 | Leg Extension | 3 | 12-15 | 60 sec | Pause at top |
| 6 | Standing Calf Raises | 4 | 12-15 | 60 sec | Full ROM, pause at top |

### Legs Day Details

#### 1. Barbell Back Squat
- **Primary:** Quads, glutes
- **Secondary:** Hamstrings, core
- **Cues:**
  - Bar on upper traps (high bar)
  - Break at hips and knees together
  - Knees track over toes
  - Depth: Hip crease below knee
  - Drive through whole foot
- **Progression:** Add 5kg when you hit 4×8

#### 2. Romanian Deadlift (RDL)
- **Primary:** Hamstrings, glutes
- **Secondary:** Lower back
- **Cues:**
  - Soft knee bend (fixed throughout)
  - Push hips back, not down
  - Feel stretch in hamstrings
  - Bar stays close to legs
  - Stop when you feel max stretch
- **Progression:** Add 5kg when you hit 3×10

#### 3. Leg Press
- **Primary:** Quads
- **Secondary:** Glutes
- **Cues:**
  - Feet shoulder width, mid-platform
  - Lower until 90° knee angle
  - Don't lock out knees at top
  - Keep lower back pressed into pad
- **Progression:** Add 10kg when you hit 3×12

#### 4. Leg Curl (Lying or Seated)
- **Primary:** Hamstrings
- **Cues:**
  - Control the weight
  - Full contraction at top
  - Slow negative (3 seconds)
  - Don't let hips rise (lying)
- **Progression:** Add 2.5kg when you hit 3×12

#### 5. Leg Extension
- **Primary:** Quads (rectus femoris)
- **Cues:**
  - Adjust pad to sit above ankle
  - Full extension at top
  - Pause and squeeze 1 second
  - Control the descent
- **Progression:** Add 2.5kg when you hit 3×15

#### 6. Standing Calf Raises
- **Primary:** Gastrocnemius
- **Cues:**
  - Full stretch at bottom (2 sec hold)
  - Rise onto big toe
  - Full contraction at top (1 sec hold)
  - Straight legs throughout
- **Progression:** Add 5kg when you hit 4×15

## Rest Day

**Purpose:** Recovery and muscle growth

### Recommendations

- **Active recovery:** Light walking, stretching, mobility work
- **Nutrition:** Maintain protein intake (1.6-2.2g per kg bodyweight)
- **Sleep:** Aim for 7-9 hours
- **Hydration:** Continue normal water intake

### Optional Activities

- 20-30 min light cardio (walking, cycling)
- Foam rolling / mobility routine
- Yoga or stretching
- Complete rest if fatigued

## Progressive Overload Strategy

### The Double Progression Method

1. **Start** at the lower end of the rep range
2. **Add reps** each session until you hit the top of the range for all sets
3. **Add weight** and drop back to the lower end of the rep range
4. **Repeat**

### Example: Bench Press (4 sets × 6-8 reps)

| Week | Set 1 | Set 2 | Set 3 | Set 4 | Weight |
|------|-------|-------|-------|-------|--------|
| 1 | 6 | 6 | 6 | 5 | 80kg |
| 2 | 7 | 6 | 6 | 6 | 80kg |
| 3 | 8 | 7 | 7 | 6 | 80kg |
| 4 | 8 | 8 | 7 | 7 | 80kg |
| 5 | 8 | 8 | 8 | 8 | 80kg ✓ |
| 6 | 6 | 6 | 6 | 5 | 82.5kg |

### Weight Increment Guidelines

| Exercise Type | Increment |
|---------------|-----------|
| Barbell compounds (Squat, Deadlift) | 5kg |
| Barbell upper (Bench, Row, OHP) | 2.5kg |
| Dumbbell exercises | 2kg (each hand) |
| Cable/machine exercises | 2.5kg |

## Warm-Up Protocol

### General Warm-Up (5 minutes)

1. Light cardio (rowing, bike, or treadmill walk)
2. Arm circles, leg swings, hip circles

### Specific Warm-Up (Before Main Lift)

| Set | % of Working Weight | Reps |
|-----|---------------------|------|
| 1 | Bar only | 10 |
| 2 | 50% | 8 |
| 3 | 70% | 5 |
| 4 | 85% | 3 |
| 5 | Working weight | Start sets |

### Example: Bench Press (Working weight: 80kg)

1. Empty bar (20kg) × 10
2. 40kg × 8
3. 55kg × 5
4. 70kg × 3
5. Begin working sets at 80kg

## Deload Protocol

### When to Deload

- Every 4-6 weeks of hard training
- When progress stalls for 2+ weeks
- When feeling excessively fatigued
- After hitting a PR goal

### Deload Options

**Option 1: Volume Deload**
- Same weight, reduce sets by 50%
- Example: 4 sets → 2 sets

**Option 2: Intensity Deload**
- Same sets/reps, reduce weight by 40-50%
- Example: 80kg → 50kg

**Option 3: Full Rest**
- Take 3-5 days completely off
- Light walking and stretching only

## Tracking Guidelines

### What to Log in Clean Gains

For each set, record:
- ✅ Weight used (kg)
- ✅ Reps completed
- ✅ Whether it felt easy/moderate/hard (optional note)

### Notes to Add

Good things to note:
- "Left shoulder tight" — helps identify patterns
- "New PR!" — celebrate progress
- "Felt strong today" — correlate with sleep/nutrition
- "Reduced rest time" — track conditioning improvements
